My research investigates the capacity of political entrepreneurs to influence voting, media consumption, and public opinion with both digital and conventional tools. I am interested in the power (and constraints) of institutions and individuals to structure awareness, beliefs, and narratives about the economy.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Politics at NYU and a Research Associate at the Social Media and Political Participation Lab. My ongoing work includes predictions of economic sentiment around the world with disciplined approaches to model selection inspired by machine learning appraoches. With my collaborators at NYU I also study the trajectories of voters’ beliefs by using survey data augmented with behavioral outdomes.

My academic research has also contributed to the debate on the threat from populism.
